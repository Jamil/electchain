About
Right now, ElectChain uses two data sources; the AP, which publishes race calls directly to the blockchain, and MIT, which publishes their data online. To show the AP data, we make direct calls to the smart contract to retrieve the data, and then visualize it in the browser; it never hits ElectChain's servers.

ElectChain publishes the MIT data to the blockchain ourselves. We maintain the initial version of the Smart Contract for recording Presidential Election results. It's expensive to get everything on the Ethereum main network, however, with the amount of data we would like to store; for each election, it costs around $200 to store all the information on the blockchain. For now, most of the elections are stored on the Ropsten test network, but we intend on migrating all results to the main network. Funds will be raised through a combination of donations and NFT sales.
